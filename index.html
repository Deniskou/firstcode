I started my life in a small French town, Villefranche de Rouergue. A place that I call a small country side town even if the meaning is a little bit paradoxical. A town of approximately 13000 inhabitants, if we count the commune. It was founded during the XIII century. You can imagine, how it was more charming and picturesque at this period. I was born there at the XX century, in 1978. At this date the town was already really less attractive. It’s at the number 15 of Félix avenue that I was bring up. One of the first workers plot of this town built in 1957. 57 identical houses, distributed in 7 parallel and perpendicular rows at the Félix avenue. The only variation is the fact that all these houses were stick on a symmetric way. From inside the house, I was easily able to imagine the neighbourhood like in a mirror of my own intimacy. The depth of the resonant coming from the wall let me at each moment supposing at which place, in which room my neighbour were. Everything, in my way of looking it was symmetrically identical. Several juxtaposed mirrors, parallels, perpendiculars, 57 exactly. The only pictures which were accessible for my sight, different than all this regular urbanisation was coming from the windows of my bedroom.
